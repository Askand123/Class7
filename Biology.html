    Plant Tissues

    book PDF of biology chapter - 1, Plant Tissues

    written work of Plant Tissues

    Summary

    Facts

    • A meristematic tissue is a group of cells that are capable of active cell division
    • A permanent tissue is a group of cells in which growth has stopped permanently or for a time
    • In leaves, the epiermis has special cells called gaurd cells that control the opening and closing of stomata
    • In big trees the outer covering of the tree trunk is multilayered and called periderm
    • If the tissue is sliced at right angle to its length, we get a transverse section (T.S.)
    • If the tissue is sliced along its length we get longitudnal section (L.S.)
    • Sclereids are reduced form of sclerenchyma cells with thickened cellular walls
    • Sclereids form a major part of the shell of nuts such as wallnuts
    • new xylem tissue keeps getting added to a growing plant as a result of actively dividing cells in the cambium, due to increase and decrease growth activity, in spring and autum, alternative pattern of growth are seen in xylem tissue of big trees.

    Complete summary

    • A cell is a basic structural and functional unit of life in all livving beings
    • The group of similar cells is known as tissues
    • There are two types of plant tissues, they are- Meristematic tissue and Permanent Tissues
    • The Group of actively dividing cells is known as Meristematic tissue
    • There are two types of meristemati tissue they are- Apical Meristematic tissue and Lateral Meristematic Tissue
    • The cells in which growth has stopped permanently or for a time is known as Permanent Tissues
    • There are two types of permanent tissue, they are- Simple permanent tissue and Complex permanent Tissue
    • Simple permanent tissue is composed of cells that functionally and structrally similar
    • there are two types of Simple permanent tissue - Protective tissue and supporting tissue
    • There are three types of dupporting tissue - Parenchyma, Collenchyma and sclerenchyma
    • Complex permanent tissue is also known as conducting tissue
    • complex permanent tissue is composed of two or more types of cells that are specialied to perform a common function
    • There are two types of complex permanent tissue- Xylem and phloem
    • Xylem is a water conducting tissue of Plant
    • phloem is a food conducting tissue of plant
    • Xylem and pholem together constitute vascular system of Plant
